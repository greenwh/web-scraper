Professional/Medical Relations Officers In Your Area

Professional Relations Officers are available in each State, or other location, for you to contact about Social Security disability programs. They make informational presentations to the professional community about Social Security's need for timely, accurate, and complete medical information, educating doctors, hospital staff, and other health professionals about how that information is used to evaluate disability.

Professional Relations Officers also recruit and train physicians (medical or osteopathic doctors), psychologists, and other health professionals to become consultative examination (CE) providers for States. They can answer your questions about performing CEs or reviewing disability claims for the disability determination services in your area. If you would like more information on performing CEs for the State DDS, please reach out to the appropriate contact in the state(s) for which you are licensed.

The Professional Relations Officers at your state's DDS can assist you with signing on to use Social Security's Electronic Records Express secure website for sending health or school records related to disability claims. You can also fax records to your state DDS or Social Security following the instructions in the letter you received requesting information.
